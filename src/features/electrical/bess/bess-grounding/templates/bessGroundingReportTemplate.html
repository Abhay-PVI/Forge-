Introduction

{{clientName}} is developing {{projectCapacityMW}} MW / {{projectCapacityMWh}} MWh of {{projectName}} of Grid Connected Standalone BESS in {{county}}, in the state of {{state}}, {{country}}. The BESS will be designed for {{projectDurationHours}} hours of continuous power output of {{projectCapacityMW}} MW at {{powerFactor}} {{powerFactorLeadLag}} power factor at the point of interconnection, resulting in {{projectCapacityMWh}} MWh of net usable battery capacity.

Standard References

This grounding report describes the data used to model the grounding systems for the {{projectName}} plant and provides an analysis of the performance of the ground grids to maintain safe tolerable step and touch voltages under faulted conditions. The primary standards referenced in this analysis are:

  1. IEEE Std 80 — IEEE Guide for Safety in AC Substation Grounding.

  4. IEEE Std C2 (NESC) — National Electrical Safety Code.

Grounding System

Battery Container, PCS Skid Grounding

The {{groundConductorBess}} equipment grounding conductor from each BESS Enclosure is connected to the {{groundingMatConductor}} grounding mat. The {{groundConductorPcs}} equipment grounding conductor from each PCS Skid is also connected to the {{groundingMatConductor}} grounding mat. The MV primary distribution cables, which consist of aluminum conductors and copper concentric neutral wires, are buried underground. The concentric neutral wires are bonded at both ends of the cable and at each transformer's grounding bus bar, which is buried at a depth of {{concentricNeutralBondingDepth}}. Additionally, there are {{groundRodSpec}} copper-clad steel grounding rods installed at regular intervals along the mat.
