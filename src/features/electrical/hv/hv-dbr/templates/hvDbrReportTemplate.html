Flexible Conductors

Flexible Conductor

Selection of flexible conductors for high voltage applications is governed by two factors: Continuous Current Rating and Short Circuit Rating. The flexible conductor sizing is designed in accordance with procedures presented in ANSI/IEEE Standard 738-2012 and IEEE 605-2008. The Flexible Conductor Sizing calculations provide the ampacity & short circuit withstand capability of the selected flexible conductor, so that it can be considered depending upon the substation requirements.

Flexible conductor sizing for continuous current rating is mainly based on the heat balance equation, which states that the temperature of a conductor depends on the balance of heat input and output. For balance, the heat input due to ohmic losses (I²R) and solar radiation equals the heat output due to convection, radiation, and conduction.

Damping Cable and Selection

Damping cables are essential to prevent horizontal cable movement within the tubing, particularly in buses where excessive vibration is anticipated. To ensure effective vibration control, damping cables should extend the entire length of the bus and be made from the same material as the bus conductor. This is critical to avoid galvanic corrosion, which can occur when dissimilar metals are in contact. Consequently, all horizontal bus runs should have damping conductors installed to maintain system integrity.

Line to the Utility Substation

This line will be protected by a line current differential scheme using a {{linePrimaryRelayModel}} for primary protection (87L-M){{linePrimaryRelayPartNo}}. 69kV Breaker

69kV Breaker {{hvBreakerTagFull}} will have high side phase instantaneous overcurrent, phase and ground time overcurrent, undervoltage, overvoltage, underfrequency, and breaker failure protection provided by {{hvBreakerRelayModel}} relay ({{hvBreakerRelayPartNo}}). 69/34.5kV Transformer

Each transformer will be primarily protected by differential protection (ANSI device 87T) and restricted earth/ground fault (REF), extending from the HV transformer bushing CTs down to the LV transformer bushing CT. The main power transformers will have protection implemented in two of the following relays: {{transformerMainRelayModel}} ({{transformerMainRelayPartNo}}) and {{transformerBackupRelayModel}} ({{transformerBackupRelayPartNo}}). Main protection lockout (86T-M) will trip TC1 and block closing of the HV/MV circuit breakers. Backup protection lockout (86T-B) will trip TC2 and block closing of the HV/MV circuit breakers.

34.5kV Bus

34.5kV bus will be protected by a differential scheme using a {{busRelayModel}} ({{busRelayPartNo}}). MV bus differential protection shall use high impedance relays connected to feeder side CTs and transformer low side CTs closest to the core. In the event of bus differential protection elements operating, a trip signal shall be issued to the corresponding lockout relay (86B-MV), which trips TC1, TC2, and blocks closing of the HV and MV circuit breakers.

34.5kV Feeders and Associated Breakers
